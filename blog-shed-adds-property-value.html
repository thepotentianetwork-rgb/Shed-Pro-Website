Does a Quality Custom Shed Add Value to Your Home? Here's What We Found.

June 2025 • 7 min read • By ShedPro LLC

Most homeowners think of a shed as a place to store stuff. Useful, sure — but not exactly an investment. What if that assumption is wrong?

As we've built hundreds of custom sheds across Utah and Idaho, we started hearing something from customers who had gone through home appraisals or sold their homes after building with us: their shed was being counted as added value. Not a small amount, either. We decided to dig into why — and what the difference is between a shed that adds value and one that doesn't.

"The appraiser walked out back, looked at the shed, and said it was a permanent structure with real value. We got $18,000 added to our appraisal." — ShedPro LLC customer, Draper, Utah

What Appraisers Actually Look For

Here's what most people don't know: appraisers don't value all sheds equally. In fact, a cheap prefab shed from a big box store typically adds zero appraised value to your home. It's treated as personal property — like furniture — not as a permanent structure.

What changes that calculation is permanence, quality, and integration with the property. Appraisers look for the following signals when deciding whether to assign value to an outbuilding:

Concrete Foundation

A poured concrete slab signals permanence. It's not going anywhere. Appraisers treat structures on concrete as real property, not personal property.

Quality Siding & Paint

LP SmartSide engineered wood siding and Sherwin-Williams paint demonstrate long-term durability — the same materials used on homes, not cheap sheds.

Metal or Quality Shingle Roof

A metal roof or premium asphalt shingles with a 40-year warranty show the structure is built to last — a key factor in assigned value.

Quality Doors & Windows

Solid entry doors, double doors, and exterior-rated windows elevate the structure. They signal a finished, livable-quality build rather than basic storage.

Electrical Wiring

Pre-wired electrical with outlets, lighting, and a power inlet makes the structure functional — not just storage. This adds measurable utility value.

Proper Framing

Pressure treated lumber framed 16" on center — the same standard as a home — demonstrates structural integrity that cheap shed kits can't match.

Quality Shed vs. Cheap Shed — Why It Matters

Not all sheds are created equal when it comes to property value. Here's the difference between a build that appraisers recognize and one they ignore:

❌ Prefab / Kit Shed

  • Factory-built with cheap OSB or T1-11 siding
  • No permanent foundation — sits on skids or gravel
  • Standard asphalt shingles or cheap metal panels
  • No electrical, no insulation
  • Warps, rots, or fades within 5–10 years
  • Treated as personal property by appraisers
  • Adds $0 to appraised home value

✅ ShedPro Custom Build

  • Built on-site with LP SmartSide engineered siding
  • Poured concrete slab — permanent structure
  • Metal roof or 40-year asphalt shingles
  • Pre-wired electrical, quality doors & windows
  • Pressure treated floor, framed 16" on center
  • Recognized as permanent outbuilding by appraisers
  • Adds real, measurable value at appraisal and resale

What Buyers Are Actually Looking For

Beyond the appraisal number, there's the buyer appeal factor — and this is where a quality shed really earns its keep. In the current Utah real estate market, a home with a well-built outbuilding stands out. Buyers scrolling through listings stop when they see a professional-looking shed in the backyard photos. It signals storage, workspace, potential rental income, or just extra square footage to work with.

Real estate agents across the Wasatch Front consistently tell us that a quality outbuilding is one of the few backyard features that actually drives buyer interest — not just at showing, but in offers. It's a differentiator in a crowded market.

"Two offers came in above asking. Our agent said the shed was a big part of why — buyers saw it and immediately started thinking about what they'd use it for."

The Bottom Line

A cheap shed is an expense. A quality shed — built on a concrete foundation, with premium siding, real roofing, solid doors and windows, and proper electrical — is an investment. It adds usable space, improves your daily life while you're in the home, and pays back a significant portion of its cost when you sell.

It's also worth noting that this isn't hypothetical. We've had customers come back to us after appraisals and closings with the actual numbers. The pattern is consistent: quality materials and permanent installation = recognized value.

If you're thinking about building a shed and wondering whether it's "worth it" — the answer, for a quality build, is almost always yes.
